## Executive Summary

The Bodzia connection is presented as a **testable hypothesis**, not a proven claim. The hypothesis states that our common ancestor (~975 CE) may have been part of the same elite network as those buried at the Bodzia cemetery (950-1020 CE). This hypothesis is based on:

1. **Phylogenetic connection:** VK157 (Bodzia) has Y-DNA I1-S2077, which connects to I-Y45113 (our branch)
2. **Timeframe match:** I-Y45113 formation (~975 CE) = Bodzia active period (950-1020 CE)
3. **Historical context:** Elite networks connecting Scandinavia, Rus', and Poland during Viking Age

**Important:** This requires comparison with published Bodzia ancient DNA data to test. Current evidence is phylogenetic (tree structure), not direct genetic match.

## What is Bodzia?

### Archaeological Site
- **Location:** Central Poland
- **Type:** Elite cemetery
- **Active Period:** 950-1020 CE (late 10th to early 11th century)
- **Excavation:** 2007-2009, led by Andrzej Buko (Polish Academy of Sciences)
- **Graves:** 58+ graves (including cenotaphs)
- **Area:** 3 hectares excavated, 2069 important features discovered

### Historical Significance
- **Elite burial ground** with rich grave goods
- Shows connections between **Scandinavia, Rus', and Piast Poland**
- Represents **cross-cultural interaction** during Viking Age
- Evidence of **long-distance connectivity** and elite mobility
- **Hybrid material culture** combining Scandinavian, Rus', and local elements

### Key Individuals
- **VK154:** Elite female, mtDNA H1c3
- **VK155:** Elite female, mtDNA H1c (same grave as VK157, maternal-line cousin)
- **VK156:** Elite male, Y-DNA R1a1a1b1a2a2a1
- **VK157:** Elite male, Y-DNA I1a3a1 (I1-S2077), mtDNA H1c
  - Proposed identification: **Sviatopolk I of Kiev** (hypothesis, not proven)
  - Same grave (E864/I) as VK155 and unexamined female

### Key Insight

- This means VK157 and our common ancestor share a **more ancient common ancestor**
- The connection is through the **phylogenetic tree structure**, not direct descent
